Where to go

The key to planning the perfect trip is picking the places that interest you. Are you looking for history, food, beaches, or all three? Know your goals for the trip before you start planning. Here are some suggestions of some personal favorites of mine.

London

London is a classic. Full of historical sights, great modern culture, and fun activities, there's so much to do in this beautiful city. Best sights and activites include Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, the Changing of the Gaurd, Covent Garden market, Abbey Road's iconic crosswalk, and the pubs.

Paris

The city of love is a must see when you're in Europe. Best attractions include The Eiffel Tower, Notre Dame Cathedral, the Louvre Museum, Luxembourg Gardens, Marché Aligre market, Les Trois Frères bar, and an evening walk down any of this beautiful city's romantic streets.

Barcelona

This iconic Spanish city is a backpacker's heaven. Parque Guell, the Sagrada Familia, the Barri Gotic bars Luz de Gas nightclub, Tibidabo view, and historic ruins of Museu d’Historia de la Ciutat are some of the best places to visit.

Get ready...

Preparations

Though backpacking through Europe is one of the cheapest, most thorough, and most authentic ways to experience the amazing continent, a lot of planning has to go into your trip if you want it to be a success.

Packing

Packing for a backpacking trip is very important. You only have one backpack and you have to carry it everywhere so overpacking is the worst thing you can do. That being said, you want to be prepared and make sure you have everything you'll need. As far as clothing goes, make sure you dress for the weather. Not being warm enough or being freezing the whole time can ruin a trip. A water bottle is a must! Good walking shoes or boots if your trip is outdoorsy are a great investment. Other good staples are a sleeping bag, a camera, and travel size toiletries. Some last, helpful tips are to practice a certain order to your packing to make it easy and quick and to leave yourself a little room so you have room for any souvenirs you might find along the way.

A Place to Stay

While backpacking, there are lots of options for places to stay. You can camp, stay in a hotel, or crash in a hostel. Since backpacking is normally an option for those on a budget, hostels are a great cheap option. Keep in mind though, that they are cheap for a reason. Most are clean enough and a reasonable option. To check, there are websitess with hostel ratings so you can check out the ones near your destinations. Hotels are always good as well, though the priciest option. Camping is a great, fun alternative if your trip is outdoorsy.
